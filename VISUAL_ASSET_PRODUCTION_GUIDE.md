## 1. THE 3 PRODUCTION COMMANDMENTS

To permanently dismantle corporate franchise competitors (e.g., Baskin Robbins) and make warehouse-frozen ice cream look like "factory-extruded plastic," all visual assets must strictly adhere to three non-negotiable creative mandates:

1. **The Spatula Mandate (*La Spatola*):**  
   **Never photograph a round, spring-loaded ice cream ball scoop.** Commercial ice cream parlors use mechanical scoops that gouge and crush dairy. Authentic Northern Italian gelato is dense, silky, and served exclusively with a flat stainless steel *spatula*. All photography must capture the *swiping, sculpting, crowning, and stretching* of gelato peaks.
2. **The Organic Sensory Palette:**  
   Eliminate harsh studio flash and clinical fluorescent lighting. Capture natural food textures: the subtle, organic sage green of pure Sicilian Bronte pistachio (explicitly proving zero artificial neon green dyes), the glistening dark fruit acids of wild Amarena cherries, and the rich golden crema of freshly extracted LaVazza espresso.
3. **Contextual Grounding in Surf City DNA:**  
   Never shoot products on generic, isolated studio backdrops. Every lifestyle and hero image must ground the product in **Huntington Beach**. Frame shots with concrete Pier pilings, palm frond shadows, wooden boardwalk planks, and surfboards resting against warm brick patio railings.

## 3. POST-PRODUCTION & WEB OPTIMIZATION SPECS

To maintain our **Mobile-First Zero-Latency Build (Phase 4)**, all raw media assets produced by the photography team must be processed through the following development pipeline before deployment:

* **Image Compression:** Convert all raw TIFF/PNG files to modern **WebP** format at 85% quality factor.
* **Responsive Breakpoint Sizing:** Deliver 3 scaled variants per image asset:
  * `*-large.webp` (1200px width for desktop displays)
  * `*-medium.webp` (800px width for tablet viewports)
  * `*-small.webp` (480px width for mobile smartphone screens)
* **Video Encoding:** Encode hero background headers to **MP4 (H.264 / AAC)** and **WebM (VP9)** with bitrate capped at 3.5 Mbps to guarantee under 1-second load times on 4G beach cellular networks.
